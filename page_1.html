About sea slugs

Sea slugs

What are they?

Sea slugs are molluscs. This family includes snails, octopus, squid, bivalves, clams and numerous other animals. They are invertebrates, so they have no backbone or internal skeleton. Despite lacking a single brain in the sense that vertebrates possess one, molluscs’ behaviour can be surprisingly complex. Slugs are a class of species within the Mollusca phylum. Some of us think of slugs as rather unpleasant animals, yet many are fascinating and beautiful. Sea slugs are the most fascinating and beautiful of all.
